Emma Dennis Reflections

I have taken an HTML class before, but that was before HTML5 had been revealed. How awesome that we have a new way to structure pages that doesn't involve div after div after div, which got confusing and cluttered very quickly. This is by far my biggest take-away and something I would like to experiment with more.

An additional new concept to me is extending HTML with server-side and client-side scripting. I am familiar with basic scripting and API's, this seems like a big topic and we just got an intro so I am looking forward to delving into this more in future classes.

SEO is also an interesting topic, I understood the basics about how different browsers gather and compile information. I found that my personal web browsing seemed to become more difficult at some point and I realize now it was due to SEO that I was unable to find things I used to be able to locate on the internet. Good to know not only for marketing and business purposes, but also for personal internet searching.
